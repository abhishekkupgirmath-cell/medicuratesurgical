Joe Burrow’s Turf Toe Injury Explained on ESPN

Sep 18, 2025 • Sports Medicine

When Cincinnati Bengals quarterback Joe Burrow suffered a "turf toe" injury, ESPN turned to Human Anatomy VR to explain the severity of the condition to fans worldwide. The segment showcased the incredible detail of the metatarsophalangeal (MTP) joint.

By isolating the ligaments and tendons of the great toe, the broadcast demonstrated how a hyperextension injury affects an athlete's ability to "push off" and accelerate. The immersive 3D models provided a clarity that traditional 2D diagrams simply cannot match.

The segment was praised for its educational value, bridging the gap between professional sports and advanced medical visualization. It highlighted how Medicurate Surgical's tools are being used beyond the classroom and clinic, reaching millions in the mainstream media.

This appearance marks another milestone in the integration of VR technology into daily information consumption and high-stakes reporting.
